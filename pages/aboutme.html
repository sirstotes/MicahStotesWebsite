Get to Know Me

Hey there,

I go by Micah Stotesbery, I'm an actor based in Salt Lake City, and my favorite animal is a raccoon even though I've only ever seen a real one from 20 yards away on a dark bike path at 11pm. Whether it's a unique accent, tight harmonies, or complicated memorization, I excel at the highly technical aspects of performance. I've been told I think like a director, and I tend to agree with that: my brain is constantly in motion, coming up with ideas and solutions to problems.

I mostly have experience with live theatre, but recently I have begun acting in film as well. In my roles, I try to bring authenticity and parts of myself to the character, which usually means I accidentally imbue them with queer subtext. I often play the outsider character, someone who doesn't quite fit in or becomes the antagonist. But I believe that any villain in a story has layers you can empathize with and learn from.

I've always had trouble connecting with my peers, except for through the performing arts. I'm very interested in how performance can empower individuals and spark connection between people of different backgrounds. As a hobbyist computer programmer, my dream is to bring live performance to lonely people through technology and the internet, and I believe that we have only begun to scratch the surface of what our modern technology is capable of.

When I'm not performing, I spend my time working on various programming projects, drawing, crocheting, or playing indie games. If any of this interests you (not the indie game part), consider subscribing to my YouTube channel or joining my mailing list to stay updated with my work and projects.
